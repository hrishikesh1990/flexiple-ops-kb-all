# Interview Process

*Flexiple Operations Knowledge Base | Category B: Process SOPs*

**Owner:** Hrishikesh | **Last Updated:** 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

The Flexiple Interview is our internal vetting step. Its purpose is to validate the candidate's skills, communication, and fit before presenting them to a client.

---

## Must-Haves

- [ ] Schedule the interview within 24-48 hours of candidate expressing interest
- [ ] Review the candidate's resume/profile before the call
- [ ] Cover: tech skills validation, communication assessment, availability, rate expectations
- [ ] Take structured notes during the call using the standard template
- [ ] Make a clear **Go / No-Go** decision within 24 hours of the interview
- [ ] Update the system with interview outcome and notes

## Good-to-Haves

- Prepare 2-3 role-specific technical questions
- Assess problem-solving approach, not just knowledge
- Gauge the candidate's enthusiasm and reliability
- Ask about past freelancing/contracting experience (relevant for contractor roles)

## Great-to-Haves

- Share feedback with the candidate regardless of outcome
- Build rapport - a rejected candidate today might be perfect for a future role
- Note any specific clients or role types the candidate would be ideal for

---

## Interview Structure (30 minutes)

| Time | Section | What to Cover |
|---|---|---|
| 0-5 min | Intro | Introduce Flexiple, explain the process, set expectations |
| 5-15 min | Tech Deep-Dive | Validate primary tech stack, discuss projects, ask situational questions |
| 15-22 min | Soft Skills | Communication clarity, work style, collaboration approach |
| 22-28 min | Logistics | Availability, rate, preferred engagement type, timezone |
| 28-30 min | Wrap-Up | Next steps, timeline, answer candidate's questions |

---

## Do's

- **Do** be punctual - start on time, end on time
- **Do** let the candidate talk more than you (70/30 rule)
- **Do** ask follow-up questions - go deeper on interesting answers
- **Do** be transparent about Flexiple's process and timeline

## Don'ts

- **Don't** conduct interviews without reviewing the candidate's background first
- **Don't** ask discriminatory questions (age, family, religion, etc.)
- **Don't** make promises about specific roles or rates
- **Don't** skip the interview because the resume "looks good" - always validate in person

---

## Good Example

> *"Tell me about the most challenging technical problem you solved in your last project. What was the approach, and what was the outcome?"*
> *(Open-ended, invites storytelling, reveals problem-solving ability)*

## Bad Example

> *"Do you know React? How many years? What about Node.js?"*
> *(Closed questions, no depth, doesn't assess real capability)*
