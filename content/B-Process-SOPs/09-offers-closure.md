# Offers & Closure

*Flexiple Operations Knowledge Base | Category B: Process SOPs*

**Owner:** Hrishikesh | **Last Updated:** 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

The offer and closure stage is where pipeline converts to revenue. Speed, clarity, and alignment between candidate and client are critical.

---

## Must-Haves

- [ ] Confirm the **offer details** with both client and candidate before formalising
- [ ] Verify: role title, start date, rate/salary, engagement type, duration, working hours
- [ ] Get the candidate's **verbal acceptance** before the client issues a formal offer
- [ ] Track the offer stage in the pipeline system
- [ ] Inform your Senior OM when an offer is being made
- [ ] Ensure all paperwork is completed within 3 business days of acceptance

## Good-to-Haves

- Pre-align expectations: check with the candidate about their expected offer before the client extends one
- Have a backup candidate ready in case the primary candidate declines
- Understand the candidate's competing offers (if any) to assess risk

## Great-to-Haves

- Negotiate win-win terms (don't just push for the highest rate - balance candidate satisfaction and client budget)
- Build a "closure playbook" for common objections (notice period, competing offers, rate mismatch)

---

## Do's

- **Do** act fast at this stage - delays lose candidates
- **Do** be the bridge between client and candidate - smooth communication is your job
- **Do** confirm everything in writing after verbal agreement
- **Do** celebrate closures with your team - it builds morale

## Don'ts

- **Don't** pressure candidates into accepting offers they're not comfortable with
- **Don't** share the client's budget with the candidate (or the candidate's minimum with the client)
- **Don't** assume a verbal "yes" is final - follow up until paperwork is signed
- **Don't** let paperwork delays kill a deal - escalate to your Senior OM if things are slow

---

## Closure Checklist

- [ ] Offer details confirmed with client
- [ ] Offer details confirmed with candidate
- [ ] Verbal acceptance received
- [ ] Formal offer issued by client (FTE) or Flexiple (Contractor)
- [ ] Offer signed
- [ ] Start date confirmed
- [ ] Pipeline updated to "Hired" status
- [ ] Senior OM notified
- [ ] First-day logistics communicated to candidate

---

## Good Example

> *"Hi [Client], great news - [Candidate] has verbally accepted the offer at [amount], starting March 25th. I'll send over the paperwork today. Could you confirm the reporting manager and onboarding contact from your side?"*

## Bad Example

> *"Candidate said yes. Let me know what's next."*
> *(No specifics, no proactivity, no next steps from your side)*
