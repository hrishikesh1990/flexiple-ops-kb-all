# Pipeline Management

*Flexiple Operations Knowledge Base | Category B: Process SOPs*

**Owner:** Hrishikesh | **Last Updated:** 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

Your pipeline is your business. Every role, candidate, and stage must be tracked accurately. Pipeline reviews happen regularly with your Senior OM.

---

## Pipeline Stages

```
Flexiple Shortlisting > Flexiple Interview > Client Shortlisting > Round 1 > Round 2 > Hired
```

| Stage | Definition | Owner | Expected Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Flexiple Shortlisting** | Sourcing and identifying potential candidates | OM | 2-3 days |
| **Flexiple Interview** | Internal vetting call completed | OM | 1-2 days |
| **Client Shortlisting** | Profiles shared with client, awaiting selection | OM | 2-5 days |
| **Round 1** | First client interview scheduled/completed | Client + OM | 3-5 days |
| **Round 2** | Second client interview (if applicable) | Client + OM | 3-5 days |
| **Hired** | Offer accepted and confirmed | OM | 2-3 days |

---

## Must-Haves

- [ ] Update pipeline **daily** - every candidate must be in the correct stage
- [ ] Add notes to every stage transition (why moved forward, why dropped)
- [ ] Track **drop-off reasons** when candidates exit the pipeline
- [ ] Attend weekly pipeline review with your Senior OM
- [ ] Flag roles stuck in any stage for more than 7 days

## Good-to-Haves

- Calculate conversion rates by stage (e.g., % of shortlisted candidates who get to Round 1)
- Identify bottleneck stages and discuss with your Senior OM
- Keep a "stale pipeline" alert for roles with no movement in 5+ days

## Great-to-Haves

- Build a personal dashboard tracking your pipeline health metrics
- Analyse patterns - which clients convert faster, which tech stacks are harder to fill
- Use pipeline data to prioritise your time (focus on roles closest to closure)

---

## Pipeline Hygiene Rules

| Rule | Frequency |
|---|---|
| All candidates in correct stage | Daily |
| Drop-off reasons logged | At time of drop |
| Stale roles flagged | Every 5 days |
| Pipeline review with Senior OM | Weekly |
| Pipeline data reconciliation | Monthly |

---

## Do's

- **Do** treat pipeline updates as non-negotiable - it's part of the job, not extra work
- **Do** use drop-off data to improve your sourcing and screening
- **Do** come prepared to pipeline reviews with Senior OM (know your numbers)
- **Do** flag risks early - a role going cold is better flagged at 5 days than 15

## Don'ts

- **Don't** leave candidates in incorrect stages - it distorts data for the whole team
- **Don't** skip pipeline updates because you're "busy with sourcing"
- **Don't** hide bad news - if a role is struggling, your Senior OM can help
- **Don't** keep "dead" candidates in the pipeline - archive them with reasons

---

## Performance Metrics from Pipeline

| Metric | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| **Active roles** | How many roles you're working on |
| **Candidates per role** | Sourcing depth |
| **Stage conversion rates** | Quality of screening at each step |
| **Time-to-fill** | Average days from role setup to hired |
| **Drop-off rates** | Where candidates are lost |
| **Revenue pipeline** | Potential revenue from active candidates in late stages |
