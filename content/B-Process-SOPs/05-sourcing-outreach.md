# 05 — Sourcing & Outreach

*Flexiple Operations Knowledge Base | Category B: Process SOPs*

**Owner:** Hrishikesh | **Last Updated:** 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

Sourcing is the engine of Flexiple. The quality and speed of your sourcing directly determines your pipeline health and revenue output.

---

## Must-Haves

- [ ] Source from Flexiple's internal talent pool first (existing vetted candidates)
- [ ] Use at least 2 sourcing channels per role
- [ ] Reach out to a minimum of 15-20 candidates per role within the first 48 hours
- [ ] Personalise outreach messages - reference the candidate's specific skills/experience
- [ ] Track all outreach in the system (who was contacted, when, response status)

## Good-to-Haves

- Batch similar roles together for sourcing efficiency
- Build sourcing lists by tech stack for future reuse
- Follow up with non-responders after 48-72 hours
- Keep a "warm list" of strong candidates who weren't placed previously

## Great-to-Haves

- Build relationships with top candidates even when there's no immediate role
- Track sourcing channel effectiveness (which channels yield the best response rates)
- Create tech-stack-specific Boolean search strings for faster sourcing

---

## Sourcing Channels

| Channel | Best For | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Flexiple internal pool | All roles | 1st - Always check first |
| LinkedIn | Senior/niche roles | 2nd |
| Job boards | Volume roles | 3rd |
| Referrals | High-quality passive candidates | Ongoing |

---

## Do's

- **Do** check the internal pool before going external - faster and cheaper
- **Do** mention Flexiple's value prop in outreach (vetted marketplace, quality clients)
- **Do** follow up - most responses come on the 2nd or 3rd touch
- **Do** disqualify quickly - don't spend time on candidates who clearly don't match

## Don'ts

- **Don't** copy-paste generic messages - candidates can tell
- **Don't** reach out to candidates already in another OM's active pipeline (check first)
- **Don't** skip the internal pool because "it takes time to search"
- **Don't** ghost candidates - even a quick "not a fit right now" builds goodwill

---

## Good Example

> *"Hi [Name], I came across your profile and was impressed by your work on [specific project] using React and Node.js. We have a client looking for exactly this skill set - it's a 6-month contractor engagement with a well-funded Series B startup. Would you be open to a quick chat this week?"*

## Bad Example

> *"Hi, we have a React opening. Interested? Please share your resume."*
