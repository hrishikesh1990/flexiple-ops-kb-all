# 08 — Client Communication

*Flexiple Operations Knowledge Base | Category B: Process SOPs*

**Owner:** Hrishikesh | **Last Updated:** 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

Client communication is the face of Flexiple. Every message, email, and call shapes how the client perceives our professionalism and reliability.

---

## Must-Haves

- [ ] Respond to all client messages within **4 business hours**
- [ ] Use professional, clear language - no jargon the client won't understand
- [ ] Always provide a **next step** or **timeline** in your message
- [ ] CC/loop in your Senior OM on critical communications (offer discussions, escalations)
- [ ] Confirm all verbal agreements in writing (email follow-up after calls)

## Good-to-Haves

- Proactively update clients on pipeline status (don't wait for them to ask)
- Prepare a brief agenda before client calls
- Summarise key decisions and action items after every call
- Adapt your communication style to the client's preference (some prefer Slack, others email)

## Great-to-Haves

- Anticipate client questions and address them proactively
- Build a relationship beyond transactional updates - understand their business context
- Share market insights (e.g., "This tech stack is in high demand right now, here's what we're seeing")

---

## Communication Cadence

| Scenario | Expected Response Time |
|---|---|
| New requirement received | Acknowledge within 2 hours |
| Shortlist sharing | Within 3-5 business days of role setup |
| Interview scheduling | Within 24 hours of client selecting candidates |
| Post-interview feedback | Follow up within 24 hours |
| Offer-stage communication | Same-day responses |

---

## Do's

- **Do** set expectations early ("I'll share a shortlist by Thursday")
- **Do** deliver on your commitments - if you can't, communicate the delay before the deadline
- **Do** take ownership of the process - the client should feel we're handling everything
- **Do** escalate to your Senior OM if a client is unhappy or unresponsive

## Don'ts

- **Don't** leave clients hanging - even "I'm working on it, will update by tomorrow" is better than silence
- **Don't** share candidate information without the candidate's consent
- **Don't** badmouth candidates to clients or vice versa
- **Don't** make commitments you can't keep (timelines, candidate quality, rates)
- **Don't** handle pricing escalations without your Senior OM's input

---

## Good Example

> *"Hi [Client], hope you're well. Quick update on the Senior React Developer role:*
> *- We've screened 12 candidates this week*
> *- 3 strong matches are moving to the shortlist - I'll share profiles by EOD Thursday*
> *- One candidate has 3 other offers, so speed on this one will help*
>
> *Let me know if you'd like to jump on a quick call to discuss priorities."*

## Bad Example

> *"Hey, still looking for candidates. Will share soon."*
> *(No specifics, no timeline, no value add)*
