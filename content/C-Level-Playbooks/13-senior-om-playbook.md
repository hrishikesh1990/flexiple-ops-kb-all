# Senior OM Playbook

*Flexiple Operations Knowledge Base | Category C: Level Playbooks*

**Owner:** Hrishikesh | **Last Updated:** 2026-03-12

---

## Your Role as a Senior OM

You lead a pod of OMs, own the pod's revenue output, develop your team members, and represent Flexiple to key clients. You report directly to Hrishikesh.

---

## Core Responsibilities

| Area | What's Expected |
|---|---|
| **Pod management** | Lead your pod (3-7 OMs), assign roles, balance workloads |
| **Revenue ownership** | Own and drive your pod's revenue targets |
| **Pipeline reviews** | Weekly 1:1 pipeline reviews with each OM |
| **Quality control** | Review profiles, client communications, and processes |
| **Team development** | Coach L1s and L2s, identify promotion-ready individuals |
| **Client relationships** | Own strategic client relationships, handle escalations |
| **Reporting** | Weekly pod update to Hrishikesh |

## Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| Monday | Pod standup, week planning, role assignment |
| Tue-Thu | 1:1 pipeline reviews (2-3 per day), client calls, quality reviews |
| Friday | Pod performance review, weekly update to Hrishikesh |

---

## Pod Management Principles

1. **Know your people** - Understand each OM's strengths, weaknesses, and growth areas
2. **Set clear expectations** - Every OM should know their weekly targets and priorities
3. **Remove blockers** - Your job is to help OMs succeed, not to do their work
4. **Hold accountability** - Track metrics, give feedback, course-correct early
5. **Develop talent** - Identify L1s ready for L1.5/L2 and L2s ready for Senior OM

---

## Pipeline Review Format (1:1 with each OM)

1. **Numbers check** - Active roles, candidates per stage, conversion rates
2. **Stuck deals** - Any role stalled for 5+ days? What's the plan?
3. **Quality check** - Spot-check 1-2 profiles or client messages
4. **Revenue forecast** - What's likely to close this month?
5. **Development** - One coaching point per review

---

## Escalation Handling

| Escalation Type | Action |
|---|---|
| Client unhappy with candidate quality | Review sourcing criteria, join next client call |
| Rate/budget mismatch | Negotiate with client, find middle ground |
| Candidate drops out late-stage | Activate backup, communicate transparently to client |
| OM underperforming | Direct feedback + coaching plan; escalate to Hrishikesh if no improvement |

---

## Do's

- **Do** lead by example - your OMs watch how you work
- **Do** give specific feedback (what to change, how, by when)
- **Do** protect your team's time - push back on unreasonable client demands
- **Do** share wins - celebrate closures publicly within the pod

## Don'ts

- **Don't** micromanage - trust your L2s, coach your L1s
- **Don't** skip pipeline reviews - consistency builds accountability
- **Don't** ignore underperformers - address issues within 2 weeks
- **Don't** make strategic decisions without consulting Hrishikesh
