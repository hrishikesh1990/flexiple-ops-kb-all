# 11 — L1 Playbook

*Flexiple Operations Knowledge Base | Category C: Level Playbooks*

**Owner:** Hrishikesh | **Last Updated:** 2026-03-12

---

## Your Role as an OM-L1

You are the execution engine of Flexiple. Your job is to source, screen, and move candidates through the pipeline reliably and at speed.

---

## Core Responsibilities

| Area | What's Expected |
|---|---|
| **Sourcing** | 15-20 candidates reached per role within 48 hours |
| **Screening** | Conduct Flexiple interviews, write candidate profiles |
| **Pipeline** | Update pipeline daily, attend weekly reviews |
| **Communication** | Respond to internal messages within 2 hours, client messages within 4 hours |
| **Revenue** | Work toward contractor target ([amount]) and FTE target ([amount]) |

---

## Daily Routine

| Time Block | Activity |
|---|---|
| First 30 min | Pipeline update + check messages |
| Morning | Sourcing block (focused, uninterrupted) |
| Midday | Flexiple interviews + profile writing |
| Afternoon | Client follow-ups + pipeline movement |
| End of day | Update pipeline, plan tomorrow |

---

## What "Good" Looks Like at L1

- Pipeline is always up to date
- Profiles are well-written and don't need heavy Senior OM edits
- Client shortlists go out on time (within committed timelines)
- You flag issues early instead of waiting
- You're hitting or trending toward base revenue targets

## What "Great" Looks Like at L1

- Consistently exceeding base targets
- Building reusable sourcing lists and templates
- Helping fellow L1s with tips and best practices
- Candidates and clients give positive feedback on your communication
- You're ready for L1.5 consideration

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Skipping pipeline updates** - "I'll do it later" becomes a data mess
2. **Sending profiles without proofreading** - Typos damage Flexiple's brand
3. **Not following up** - Candidates and clients go cold without regular touches
4. **Working in isolation** - Ask your pod members and Senior OM for help
5. **Optimising for volume over quality** - 5 great profiles beat 15 mediocre ones

---

## Your Growth Path

```
OM-L1 > OM-L1.5 > OM-L2
```

To move to L1.5, you need to:

- Consistently hit base targets for 3+ months
- Demonstrate ownership - your Senior OM doesn't need to chase you
- Show initiative - suggest improvements, help others
- Get positive client feedback
