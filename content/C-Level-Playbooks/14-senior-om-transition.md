# 14 — Senior OM Transition Guide

*Flexiple Operations Knowledge Base | Category C: Level Playbooks*

**Owner:** Hrishikesh | **Last Updated:** 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

This guide is for OMs transitioning to Senior OM. The shift from individual contributor to pod leader is significant - you're now responsible for other people's output, not just your own.

---

## The Mindset Shift

| As an OM (L2) | As a Senior OM |
|---|---|
| Own your pipeline | Own your pod's pipeline |
| Hit your targets | Enable your pod to hit targets |
| Solve your problems | Coach others to solve theirs |
| Execute well | Build systems for consistent execution |
| Get feedback from Senior OM | Give feedback + report to Hrishikesh |

---

## First 30 Days as Senior OM

### Week 1 - Understand Your Pod

- [ ] 1:1 meeting with each pod member - understand their strengths, current workload, and concerns
- [ ] Review each OM's pipeline and performance data from the last 3 months
- [ ] Read the [[13-senior-om-playbook|Senior OM Playbook]] thoroughly
- [ ] Set up recurring 1:1 pipeline reviews with each OM

### Week 2 - Establish Rhythm

- [ ] Run your first pod standup (Monday)
- [ ] Conduct your first round of pipeline reviews
- [ ] Identify the top 2-3 things your pod needs to improve
- [ ] Start your first weekly report to Hrishikesh

### Week 3-4 - Start Coaching

- [ ] Give each OM one specific area to improve
- [ ] Spot-check profiles and client communications (don't rewrite - give feedback)
- [ ] Handle your first client escalation (with Hrishikesh's guidance if needed)
- [ ] Identify any L1s who might be ready for L1.5 consideration

---

## Common Transition Pitfalls

1. **Doing the work instead of managing** - You'll be tempted to jump in and "fix" things. Resist. Coach instead.
2. **Being too nice** - Accountability requires uncomfortable conversations. Have them early.
3. **Ignoring your own pipeline** - If you still carry roles, don't let management duties kill your execution.
4. **Not asking for help** - Hrishikesh and other Senior OMs are there to support you. Use them.
5. **Trying to change everything at once** - Pick 1-2 improvements and execute them well.
