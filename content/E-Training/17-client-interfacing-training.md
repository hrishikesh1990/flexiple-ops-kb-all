# 17 — Client Interfacing Training

*Flexiple Operations Knowledge Base | Category E: Training*

**Owner:** Hrishikesh | **Last Updated:** 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

Client calls and messages are where Flexiple's reputation is built. This training module covers how to prepare, conduct, and follow up on client interactions.

---

## Before the Call

### Must-Haves
- [ ] Review the client's open roles and pipeline status
- [ ] Prepare a brief agenda (3-4 bullet points)
- [ ] Have candidate profiles ready if sharing shortlists
- [ ] Test your audio/video setup

### Good-to-Haves
- Research the client's company (recent news, funding, product updates)
- Prepare 1-2 questions that show you understand their business
- Have backup candidates ready in case the client asks

---

## During the Call

### Structure (30-minute call)

| Time | Section | Tips |
|---|---|---|
| 0-3 min | Greeting + agenda | "Today I'd like to cover X, Y, Z. Sound good?" |
| 3-15 min | Updates/shortlists | Lead with the strongest candidate, give context |
| 15-25 min | Discussion | Listen more than you talk, take notes |
| 25-30 min | Next steps | Confirm action items, set timeline |

### Communication Principles

1. **Be clear** - No ambiguity. "I'll send profiles by Thursday" not "I'll send soon"
2. **Be honest** - If something is taking longer, say so. Don't hide problems.
3. **Be proactive** - Anticipate questions, offer solutions before being asked
4. **Be professional** - Avoid filler words, slang, or overly casual language
5. **Be concise** - Respect their time. Get to the point.

---

## After the Call

- [ ] Send a summary email within 2 hours (key decisions + action items)
- [ ] Update the pipeline with any new information
- [ ] Complete your action items by the committed timeline
- [ ] Share relevant notes with your Senior OM

---

## Common Scenarios

### Client is unhappy with candidate quality
- Listen fully before responding
- Acknowledge the concern: "I understand - the profiles haven't matched expectations"
- Ask specific questions: "What would the ideal candidate look like?"
- Commit to a revised approach with timeline

### Client is slow to respond
- Send a polite follow-up after 48 hours
- Offer to simplify the process: "Would it help if I shortlisted 2 instead of 5?"
- If still no response after 1 week, escalate to your Senior OM

### Client wants to renegotiate rate mid-process
- Don't commit on the call: "Let me check on our side and get back to you"
- Discuss with your Senior OM before responding
- Present any counter-proposal in terms of value, not just numbers

---

## Do's

- **Do** take notes during every call
- **Do** repeat back key decisions ("Just to confirm - you'd like to interview Candidate A and C")
- **Do** end with clear next steps and ownership

## Don'ts

- **Don't** overpromise on timelines or candidate quality
- **Don't** speak negatively about candidates
- **Don't** share Flexiple's internal processes or margin details with clients
