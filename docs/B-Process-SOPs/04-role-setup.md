# Role Setup

*Flexiple Operations Knowledge Base | Category B: Process SOPs*

**Owner:** Hrishikesh | **Last Updated:** 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

Role setup is the first step after a client shares a new requirement. Getting this right determines the quality of sourcing, shortlisting, and ultimately the placement.

---

## Must-Haves

- [ ] Capture the **job title**, **tech stack**, and **experience range** from the client
- [ ] Confirm **engagement type** - Contractor (Flexiple Payroll) or FTE (Client Payroll)
- [ ] Get the **budget range** (monthly for contractors, CTC for FTEs)
- [ ] Understand the **start date** and urgency level
- [ ] Identify **must-have skills** vs nice-to-have skills
- [ ] Enter the role into the tracking system within 24 hours of receiving it

## Good-to-Haves

- Understand the client's interview process (how many rounds, who interviews)
- Get context on the team the hire will join (team size, tech lead, reporting structure)
- Clarify timezone preferences and overlap requirements
- Ask about any candidates they've already rejected (to avoid duplicates)

## Great-to-Haves

- Understand why the role is open (growth, backfill, new project)
- Get the client's "dream candidate" profile for calibration
- Understand competing offers or other agencies the client is working with

---

## Do's

- **Do** confirm the role details in writing (email/message back to the client)
- **Do** ask clarifying questions upfront - it saves time later
- **Do** set expectations on timeline ("We'll share first shortlist within X days")
- **Do** flag any mismatch between budget and requirements to your Senior OM immediately

## Don'ts

- **Don't** start sourcing without a confirmed job description
- **Don't** assume tech stack details - always verify with the client
- **Don't** promise specific candidate availability before checking
- **Don't** skip entering the role in the system - pipeline tracking depends on it

---

## Good Example

> *"Hi [Client], thanks for sharing the requirement. Let me confirm - you're looking for a Senior React developer, 5-8 years experience, for a contractor engagement at [amount]-2L/month. Must-have: React, TypeScript, Node.js. Nice-to-have: AWS experience. Start ASAP, with 4+ hours IST overlap. I'll share a shortlist of 3-5 profiles by Thursday."*

## Bad Example

> *"Got it, will start looking for React developers."*
> *(No confirmation of details, no timeline, no budget acknowledgment)*
